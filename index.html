2020 mean: $114.94 (n=12,255)  |  2021 mean: $84.49 (n=10,974)

Critical Finding: The 2020 season ended before COVID started; 2021 was the post-COVID year. The raw price difference is significant, but OLS Model 3 shows it disappears when controlling for promotion mix (coef = +$4.55, p = 0.074, not significant). The price difference between seasons is explained by 2020 having more promotion games, not by COVID. COVID impacted ticket sales volume -- 2021 had 2,694 fewer seats and 1,281 fewer transactions -- not the underlying price level.

3. Schedule Low-Demand Opponents on Sundays

Sunday is the highest ATP day of the week; pair it with low-demand opponents to maximize revenue lift.

4. Add Promotions on Non-High-Demand Days

Weekday and low-ATP day games should receive promotional support to offset weaker demand.

5. Shift Last-Minute Buyers to Earlier Purchases

A 20% shift from Last-Minute to the Planner window generates +$155,701/season in additional secondary market revenue.

6. Segment-Based Email Targeting

Segment the mailing list by purchase behavior and tailor outreach timing accordingly.

SegmentWindow% of CustomersATPEmail Strategy
Planners15+ days44%$124Early access, presales, early-bird discounts (10-15% off)
Normal3-14 days27%$113Weekly "Upcoming Games" digest, bundle offers (tickets + parking)
Last-Minute0-2 days29%$72Day-of flash sale alerts, "Tonight Only" subject lines
Track open rates, click-through rates, and conversion by segment. A/B test subject lines and send times within each segment. Expected lift: 15-25% improvement in email-driven ticket sales through personalization.

7. Halftime Seat Upgrades

$10/section upgrade at halftime with a 1/3 take rate (I estimate based on typical upsell conversion) generates $197,690 in incremental revenue.

8. A/B Testing Framework

Run A/B tests for pricing adjustments and early-bird discounts during the first half of next season. If results validate hypotheses (no significant demand reduction, measurable shift to advance purchases), proceed with full deployment for the following season.
